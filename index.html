Perspective

How I think

01

The gap between acquisition and retention is where most marketing programs fall apart.

Most organisations celebrate getting a customer and then move on. The metrics look good, the campaign gets called a success, and attention shifts to the next acquisition push. The real work starts after the first conversion. I've spent most of my career in that gap — mapping where customers go quiet, building the journeys that keep them engaged, and finding the moments where the relationship either deepens or starts to erode.

02

Data infrastructure is a commercial decision, not a technical one.

When I led the Salesforce Data Cloud implementation at Cricket Victoria, the biggest challenge wasn't technical. It was getting internal stakeholders to understand why unified data mattered commercially — what decisions we couldn't make without it, what revenue we were leaving on the table. The technology was the easy part. Once people could see the commercial logic, the project moved. That sequence matters.

03

I built a tool because the right one didn't exist.

At Aerion I was working closely with software developers and kept running into the same problem — meetings were generating a lot of discussion and not enough useful output. So I built Jotter, an AI note-taking tool designed to keep meeting outputs and marketing collateral creation more productive. It taught me that the best solutions come from living inside the problem, not observing it from the outside. I am not a developer. I built it anyway.

04

Good agency management is not about being the client.

Most marketers treat agency relationships as a service transaction — you brief them, they deliver, you pay. I treat them as a performance partnership. I set clear targets, review results critically, ask hard questions about methodology, and replace partners when they are not delivering. That approach recovered significant budget and performance at both Cricket Victoria and Provider Plus. The best agencies respond well to it. The ones who don't are usually the problem.

Lifecycle & Retention Provider Plus

Rebuilding the member lifecycle to stop early churn

The problem

A B2B SaaS membership platform losing too many members in the first 90 days, before they'd experienced the full value of the platform. The first conversion was happening. The second wasn't.

What I did

Mapped the full member journey, identified two critical drop-off points — at onboarding and at the six-month mark — and rebuilt the HubSpot lifecycle program to address both. Triggered sequences, behavioural signals, re-engagement journeys matched to the moments that actually mattered.

Results

17% improvement in member retention
22% reduction in cost per acquisition
60%+ market share — Australian NDIS platform space
